Business Value in NFL Salary Efficiency

Project Overview

This project was the final assignment for my SQL & Databases class, where I was tasked to find a dataset and from that create a promising business opportunity. I chose to investigate salary efficiency in the NFL through SQL-based data analysis. I proposed a consulting service to help NFL front offices make smarter salary allocation decisions using wins-per-dollar dashboards, positional ROI models, and roster simulations.

The Business Idea

  • Wins-per-Dollar Dashboards – Visual tools that compare team performance to salary spend
  • Positional ROI Models – Show which positions yield the best value
  • Roster Construction Simulations – Recommend optimal spending scenarios

The Dataset

Using 2023 season data, I analyzed team wins, player salaries, and positional spending. I organized the data into an entity relationshipm diagram and proceeded to use SQL to help uncover trends in efficiency across the league’s 32 teams.

Key Findings

Most Efficient: Minnesota Vikings

  • Record: 14–3
  • Total Salary: $160.5M
  • Wins per $1M: 0.087

Least Efficient: Cleveland Browns

  • Record: 3–14
  • Total Salary: $191.3M
  • Wins per $1M: 0.016

Where to Spend

Quarterback spending showed the strongest correlation with wins:

  • High QB spenders averaged 9.06 wins
  • Low QB spenders averaged 7.94 wins
  • Defensive spending and overall team salary were less predictive of success
